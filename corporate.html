  • Font choices

User interface patterns

The structure of the navigation and content
The navigation Is designed in order to not be too harsh on the eye. The content on the pages is distributed in a balanced way: if a section presents more colors, text or pictures, the second section that follows after is more light, and not too crowded, in order for the user to not get overwhelmed by the amount of information or design. The colors give the user a funk experience. The information is placed in the middle, in order to be readable for the user and to not miss it. Clear navigation and a logical Information Architecture ensure the user can intuitively browse the website without confusion. This includes a clear hierarchy, easy-to-understand menu labels, and a search function, all of which contribute to a positive user experience by minimizing effort and frustration.

1. Navigation Bar - The design of the navigation bar is bold, and simple, especially used for a user-friendly experience, especially when the content and layout of the pages emerge colors and richness in content. Moreover, the navigation bar is sticky, which provides the user with easiness of navigation, allowing the user to navigate through other pages without having to scroll back to the top.
  • - A search bar is incorporated directly into the navigation bar, which supports autonomy, which is another element that provides the user with easy navigation, because the user can search freely the exact content that he wants, without having to scroll through the pages to find it, fitting with a user-centric design ethos. The search is time-effective, being a quicker option for the user to get the information it needs.


2. Image and Text Layout and colors - For the “product features” on the landing page section, the dynamic use of presenting information through content and images provides the user with the feeling of a concert experience, mirroring it to the back-and-forth rhythm. Moreover, the layout caters to the F-pattern reading style of web users, the information being placed in areas where viewrs naturally begin to scan the pages.
  • The website’s visual and interactive elements reflect the brand's personality and voice. Consistency in these elements ensures that the user associates the high-quality UX with the Amplify brand itself, enhancing brand recognition and loyalty. The use of brand-specific elements in the UI design (like icons, buttons, and color palettes, carousels and the herso section) makes the brand more memorable.
  • - The purple gradient background creates a different and magic experience for the user , which is memorable, reinforcing brand recognition.
  • - Moreover, the color choices reflect the boldness and passion of the hard rock genre, which aligns perfectly with the target audience- hard rock fans.


3. Call-to-action buttons: - The buttons are strategically placed, serving as a strong call to action, aligning also with our marketing goals, providing awareness, and increasing app downloads as well as reach. The buttons have the same design, red in the beginning and when clicked, they turn into yellow. Red is a strong color that drags the user’s attention to the call-to-action, and yellow is the accent color that makes it important.
  • - “Click to find out about our app” button, when clicked, it runs the user to the section where it can check and find information about the features that our app provides, instead of having to scroll down. It gives a more dynamic and interactive touch.
  • - The buttons that lead the user to download the app are strong call to action, visible and straightforward in order to push the user to get our product: the app. In this way, it is more accessible, quicker, which makes the user and the navigation effective for the user, not having to search for the app in order to download it: we give to the user the exact action to do so on the website.
  • - CTAs are strategically placed and designed with red background BUT yellow highlights to guide and take the attention of the the user towards the marketing strategy’s objectives. The design of these elements is critical—they stand out but not overpower the overall design.


4. Footer - The footers serves as a tool where users can find contact information, additional navigation link and social media link, facilitating trust and ongoing engagement, as well as the possibility for the users to keep contact on the social media and engage with our social media, keeping up the marketing phase and creating the feel of connectiveness.

5. Landing Page grid
  • The purple grid form the landing page serves an organized yet pleasing to the users’ eyes way to present information. The grid allows to present information in a simple, structured and direct way.


Our Story page grid

Bright calls to action and interactive features, such as social media connections, involve people immediately and create a feeling of community and involvement similar to attending a concert in person. The overall look, which consists of dark tones broken up by flashes of color, is reminiscent of the hard rock movement's edgy, rebellious spirit. This effectively appeals to the fervent fan base of the genre and invites people to "amplify" their live experiences together.


Our Vision section
  • The page section offers a bold, simplistic style that perfectly encapsulates the mood of hard rock. As an anchor, the title "OUR VISION" makes a strong declaration that establishes the tone for the content that follows. The writing style of the text is casual and lively, reflecting the genuine and casual way that hard rock fans may interact. The lettering is expressive and a little asymmetrical, giving it an authentic and nostalgic feel similar to hand-painted concert posters.


Our Team dynamic carousel
  • The carousel design is a dynamic user interface element that serves to engage users by showcasing multiple content items without user input, making efficient use of space and user attention. It automatically slides to offer a glimpse into the 'OUR TEAM' section, presenting team members in a manner that embodies the energy and vibrancy of the hard rock scene. The choice to auto-advance reflects a UX decision to reduce the user's cognitive load, allowing them to passively receive information without additional actions, which can be particularly effective for users accessing the site via mobile devices or users who prefer to browse content with minimal interaction. The monochrome aesthetic of the photographs against the stark, contrasting background aligns with the genre’s traditional visuals, while the splashes of brighter colors in the text and highlights bring focus to key information and maintain the user’s attention as the carousel transitions, providing a visual rhythm that echoes the pulsing beats of rock music.


Popovers with OUR VALUES
  • The "OUR VALUES" section's popovers provide an interactive and captivating approach to display content without overwhelming the user while keeping a clean, focused design. This strategy appeals to hard rock fans' exploratory spirit and offers a rewarding informational reveal similar to uncovering hidden gems, making it ideal for the target audience that values authenticity and discovery.

Context of campaign and promotional activities

Amplify is a brand that strives to connect rock fans through concerts and amplify their experience. We are a service that offers rock fans the chance to connect with other rock fans alike them and go to concerts around The Netherlands. From the conducted interviews with our target audience we found out that:

  • rock fans don’t have people to go to concerts with
  • It is hard for rock fans to find people to go to concerts with and they don’t know where to look
  • They want to meet new people in general
  • They want to have new experiences
Social Media choice

Target audience is hard rock fans aged between 18-30, who are hard rock listeners and open to listen to different sub-genres of rock, they enjoy the rush of attending concerts more than once a year. Instagram is the most used platform by people aged 18-34. They make up 61 % of all Instagram users (Zote,2024). From the conducted interviews all 8 of our interviewees use Instagram as their preferred platform. Some of them use Reddit and just 1 has Facebook, which he hasn’t opened in months , so we decided not to focus on Facebook and choose only Instagram.

Objectives
1. Reach
  • Reach 500 accounts through “accounts reached” by March 31st, 2024.
When individuals encounter a brand for the first time, they already have an idea of the brand visibility, reputation, and its message (Edelman, 2023). Therefore, trust is necessary. In this context, 500 accounts reached will give the confidence to other individuals to trust and visit the social media page.
  • An average watch time of 4 seconds per reel by March 31st, 2024
The average watch time of Instagram reels is 3-4 seconds( Temples,2024)
  • Average of 250 views per reel shared on Instagram by March 31st, 2024
The most reached followers per reel for an account up to 500 followers was 288 in 2023, so we think it would be realistic to say that we want to have an average of 250 views per shared reel.
2. Affect
  • We want our audience to feel excited and engaged by March 31st, through engagement in posts by turning on countdown reminders and notifications, interacting on polls, quiz, questions and direct messages.
Social media engagement is found to have a great impact on small businesses affecting especially the customer loyalty (Read, 2020).Research also found that 63% of consumers agree that their loyalty to a brand is influenced by the quality of customer service provided on social media (Social media’s role, n.d.)
3. Advocacy response
  • Have at least one share per post on Instagram by March 31st, 2024.

If people share the social media content that the brand is creating it can start a sense of trust and quality, because people sometimes see popular content as investigated and approved by others (Agency Analytics, n.d.)

  • Reach a minimum of 1 comment on each Instagram post by March 31st, 2024.

Furthermore, a research conducted with 123,410 Instagram profiles shows that profiles with up to 1000 followers only get 1 comment per post maximum on a day (Bieldfeldt, 2021).

  • Hit at least 10 to 15 likes per post on Instagram by March 31st, 2024.

The aforementioned research conducted by Bielfeldt (2021) and Erxleben (2021) both mention that small Instagram accounts up to 1000 followers get a max of 5 likes per day.

  • Have 20 clicks of the link in our bio by March 31st

The link in the bio will be used to send the audience to the website and therefore they will be directed to more business focused content which increases brand presence (Fuhrmann Creative; LLC, 2023, August 23).

Campaign

For our media campaign we use Instagram as a social media platform to promote the brand.

With our campaign we want our target audience to get familiar with our brand and feel being part of our hard rock fans community. We also want to spread awareness about our app.

  • We introduced our brand, shared our values and vision in a post. To help us achieve our Affect objective, because it made our audience feel excited about our brand, create a sense of loyalty. Sharing our values also help us attract customers that share the same values.
  • Countdown stories - to achieve our Affect objective, because audience will feel engaged by turning on countdowns for our launches
  • Reels - we shared behind the scenes reels to achieve our Reach objectives. We also shared our reels on stories with interactive questions about them so the audience can engage with them more.We also introduced our team in individual reels for each person.
  • Posts with information about our service and our app. These posts help us build trust in our brand, build excitement as well. They promote our brand and our service.We shared main features of the app to keep the audience engaged for the launch of the product. We also shared these posts in stories for further engagement and word of mouth recommendations.
  • For all posts and reels we used detailed and longer captions to keep the attention of the viewer to help with engagement. We also added hashtags to all posts and reels to make them more discoverable.


First photo is the first content calendar, second is the improved version of our first content calendar, after testing out different posting times.

  • Remade content calendar:

This is how our content calendar looks after the changes in time for posting and some new content ideas. We also spread out the reels posting to ensure more engagement. We added more posts that share information about our website and about our app to help promote our brand. We also added more stories with interactive polls and questions to make our audience engage more with our brand.

Learning Points

Reach

  • Reach 500 accounts through “accounts reached” by March 31st, 2024.- We reached this objective, even exceeded with almost a 1000 more accounts. We didn’t expect to achieve this objective, we actually thought that 500 accounts is too much to reach in 4 weeks.
  • An average watch time of 4 seconds per reel by March 31st, 2024- All of our reels are 5 seconds or longer, so we thought that 4 seconds average watch time would be achievable, because most people don’t watch reels until the end. We managed to achieve this objective for 4 out of 5 reels. We didn’t reach this objective on our most watched reel (1451 views), so we think that this reel had a strong hook, but could not captivate the attention of the viewers for any longer, so the reel is lacking something in the middle- adding more value, element of a surprise, call to action or highlight the benefit of watching until the end.
  • Average of 250 views per reel shared on Instagram by March 31st, 2024- During the first two weeks we had posted three reels all with under 200 views. We thought that we would not reach this objective during the last two weeks. But then one of the reels gained over a 1000 views, therefore all other reels also received engagement and went over 250 views. This was also the reel with the strongest hook, so we learned that reels with stronger hooks and longer captions receive more views. FFor this reel we also used a trendy instagram sound which we think is key for the engagement on this reel.

Affect

  • We want our audience to feel excited and engaged by March 31st, through engagement in posts by turning on countdown reminders and notifications, interacting on polls, quiz, questions and direct messages. - We believe that for 4 weeks we have built a small but strong community that believes in our brand. We posted a lot of stories with interactive polls, questions and our followers were very active on those polls. We also received a few direct messages and we tried to respond in less than an hour. In those direct messages we also received positive feedback on the look of our feed and our values as well, which we believe shows that we have built some sense of trust and loyalty in our followers. We learned that interactive stories are a great way to interact with our followers and to open discussions in direct messages so we will continue to make them and more often.

Advocacy response

  • Have at least one share per post on Instagram by March 31st, 2024.- We managed to reach this objective, all of our posts have been shared once by different people on their stories. Even though we reached this objective, we were hoping for more shares. We learned that longer captions with more value and information will be more beneficial for people to spread the word for our reels, so they share this information with others.
  • Reach a minimum of 1 comment on each Instagram post by March 31st, 2024.- We reached this objective on a few of our posts. We were expecting to reach it and even exceed it. We learned that a call to action in the caption, or adding a question in the caption that viewers can answer in the comments and start a discussion, will bring us more comments on our posts.
  • Hit at least 10 to 15 likes per post on Instagram by March 31st, 2024.- We managed to reach this objective as expected. We learned that building trust and loyalty in our audience builds a stronger community , therefore consistency in our engagement on posts and reels.
  • Have 20 clicks of the link in our bio by March 31st- We almost reached this objective. We believe that we didn’t reach it because we shared the link to our website too late. We also could have posted it on our stories with a call to action to view our website to receive more traffic.

Overall we learned that a well made and prepared Content calendar is the key to a successful campaign. We managed to post everything planned in the Content calendar, because with it you can see what you have to prepare for next week. It removed the stress from creating the content last minute or forgetting to post. Midway during our campaign we scheduled our content on the Instagram app itself (Instagram provides this feature to “pre-post”) so we got a reminder every time, you just click and post, this also helped to not forget to post. This was especially helpful to post at exactly 6pm, because this is the time we received the most engagement, after testing other posting times. We learned that reels should have strong hooks and some element of value or surprise in the middle to keep the viewer’s attention until the end. We learned that asking questions in the captions of our reels and posts will help open discussions in the comments. We received the most engagement with our profile through reels, so we learned that we should focus on them. We also learned that interactive stories are very helpful with interacting with our audience, which builds trust and loyalty.

Future Planning

If we continue the campaign in the future, we will first set new objectives, so that we have clear goals and the content we make aims to reach those objectives. We will also make a new Content calendar, because it helped us a lot during those four weeks. It removed the stress from forgetting when to post and what to post, brainstorming and planning the content ahead helped us save more time on thinking during the campaign and gave us time to interact with our audience and be more active on stories. In the future we will focus on reels, because they brought the most traffic to our page, but we will use more trendy instagram sounds so they reach more people. We will also make reels have strong hooks and more information both in the video and in the caption, because our best performing reel had both of those. For posts we will add questions that the audience can answer in the comments for further engagement and start discussions. We will post more stories with interactive questions, polls. We want to interact more with our audience so we build trust and loyalty. In the future we would also want to have posts or reels in collaboration with influencers or hard rock bands for some giveaway. We found that giveaways bring a lot of engagement to profiles. We posted our website link too late and with no previous announcement so in the future, when there are new changes in our website or app, we will notify the audience that changes will be made or that there will be new announcements( like the giveaways) so we build anticipation in our audience and further engagement.

Problem:

  • Out of all the interviews we did, which were 10 in total, these 4 problems were the most prevalent in all of them. Some we already knew, others not, but for all of them it became more clear that they pose a real problem within the hard rock community.

Existing Alternatives:

  • Social Media: It is used a lot for connecting especially Discord, Reddit, Facebook groups and Instagram can be used to directly (video) chat with people.
  • Tinder Festival Mode: Tinder has a feature called Tinder Festival Mode on which people can match who are going to the same festival or who have a love for live music
  • Appic: an app which can be used to find festivals, parties and clubs in the Netherlands and Belgium.
  • Songkicl: an app on which people can find concerts of Warner Music Group.

Solution:

  • Benefit: The app has kind of the same way of working as Tinder on which people can see and go through the account of others who are going to the same concert as them and if both persons save each other’s account they get put in a chat together to have the chance to talk to meet each other and maybe meet up at said concert. Before they see the accounts of other people, the app provides a map of the Netherlands so that people can see which concerts are coming up. When they register for one concert only then they can see the accounts of the people who are also going. If someone shows interest in another person and vice versa, only then a chat room will be created to start talking. For each concert you can have multiple chats and even combine them to create a bigger group! We know that Amplify is a solution for the problem that the Hard Rock community faces. After doing some in-depth interviews almost all of the interviewees said they would like to be engaged or more engaged within the Hard Rock community and that going to concerts is most fun with others, who can be hard to find besides people they already know. Amplify wants the Hard Rock community to be more engaging by using the concert experience as its main selling point.

Key Metrics:

  • The amount of accounts: If we know the amount of accounts registered in our app we have an idea if the app has enough users to run and if it is or can be successful.
  • The amount of chats that are created: Although we are not going to read what has been said in the chats, to know the amount of chats that have been created, we can get an insight if our main goal for the app is being met: connecting Hard Rock fans with each other. We can also see how many people directly interact with the app besides only making an account.
  • The amount of people that signed up for a concert: With the knowledge about how many people register for a concert, we can have an idea what kind of concerts get the most attention and again, we see how many people directly interact with the app besides only making an account.
  • The amount of website visitors: If we know how many people visit the website, not only the people making an account, we can get an idea of how easy we are to find and we can determine the ratio between people who make an account and who do not.
  • The amount of Instagram interactions: Because Instagram is our most important communication and promotion channel, the likes, comments, followers etc. gives a good indication if we are doing good.

Costs:

  • Fixed Costs: To create an app we need to have at least a computer and an app builder on it, otherwise we cannot make anything. Furthermore, we need a workplace to work on it, which will cost some rent. We also need to have salaries for the people we hire to help us with building and producing the app.
  • Variable Costs: An app has multiple prototypes that need to be tested, and every test can differ in the amount of money it costs. Furthermore, the app also needs advertising on multiple platforms to attract possible customers.
  • Costs per Unit: The Average costs of our startup app starts at 30.000 dollars which is 27.615 euros The amount of users differs a lot between only 10 all up to somewhere in the early 2000, so we estimated that 1000 users would be a good starting point for our app, as this is something new entirely that has not been done. Costs per Unit: 27.615 : 1000 users = 27,62 euros

Value Proposition and Business Plan:

After we conducted the problem interviews we began to look for the problems or the insights that the interviews or a lot of the interviews share with each other. We noticed that a lot of the interviewees wanted to be more connected with the people within the (Hard) Rock community and that most of them enjoy going to concerts, especially when they can share the experience of concerts with others.


We decided that we can combine the urge for more connection within the community, the love for concerts and wanting to experience concerts with others in 1 product: Amplify, the solution seen on the second and third lean canvas. We created an app on which people can find other people to go to a concert with. We want for people to seek and find like-minded Hard Rock fans and make connections while enjoying a concert. With Amplify we amplify the experience. Because all our interviews were with people who are from (early) Gen Z, we decided to focus Amplify on Hard Rock fans between 18-30 years old.


With Amplify there is also a possibility for people to discover or take interest in new music and artists, because you can see all Hard Rock concerts on 1 platform. Maybe people will go out of their comfort zone, and this can help going against the overarching problems of not knowing where to find other music and artists and the judgement within the community. But, at its core Amplify is an app to connect Hard Rock fans through concerts. If we can help tackle the other problems, that would be a nice bonus.


It is interesting to notice that the solutions we proposed in the first lean canvas came back in Amplify and that it is something that Hard Rock fans really have a problem with. The problems we thought of in the first lean canvas are real problems we saw in the problem interviews, especially the problem of wanting to meet more people in the Hard Rock community. Not knowing when concerts are was not a big problem we noticed, but concerts were said to be events to connect. Furthermore, the target audience described in the first lean canvas works really well with Amplify. We saw the descriptions from the target audience back in our interviewees. The reason that we already had a good knowledge of the Hard Rock community is because some members in the group are Hard Rock fans and they already know a bit about the community. Because of this knowledge we had a strong start from the beginning. In the end, we think that combining the first 2 solutions we thought of in the first lean canvas would be perfect for Hard Rock fans. The solutions combined were: ‘Creating a social media channel in which Hard Rock fans can chat one-on-one or in groups’, and ‘Creating an app to see when there are Hard Rock concerts in your designated area.’ Combining these 2 solutions will especially tackle the problem of wanting to connect more with fellow Hard Rock fans, but not knowing where to start. And thus: Amplify has been born.
